## 1. Brand Positioning

### 1.1 Brand Essence
**"AI that sells for you."**

Vayva is not a website builder. It is not a payment tool. It is not a chatbot. Vayva is an AI sales agent that works inside your WhatsApp, understands your products, talks to your customers in their language, and closes sales 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

### 1.2 Brand Personality
- **Confident** -- We know our product works and we are not afraid to say so
- **Local** -- We speak the language, understand the hustle, and build for Nigerian realities
- **Intelligent** -- Powered by world-class AI, but never condescending
- **Warm** -- Technology should feel like a partner, not a robot
- **Bold** -- We are building something that has never existed in Africa

### 1.3 Brand Voice Guidelines

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Social media | Conversational, confident | "Your AI agent just closed 12 sales while you slept. Good morning." |
| Merchant onboarding | Warm, encouraging | "Welcome to Vayva. Let us set up your AI agent -- it takes about 5 minutes." |
| Investor communications | Data-driven, visionary | "We are building the commerce operating system for Africa's 41.5M SMBs." |
| Support | Empathetic, solution-oriented | "We hear you. Let us fix this together right now." |
| Advertising | Bold, benefit-focused | "Stop losing sales. Start selling in your sleep." |

### 1.4 Visual Identity Principles
- **Color palette:** Modern, vibrant, African-inspired (not the typical "African tech" orange/green cliche)
- **Typography:** Clean, contemporary, highly readable on mobile
- **Imagery:** Real Nigerian merchants, real storefronts, real WhatsApp conversations
- **No stock photos:** Every image should feel authentic to the Nigerian business experience

## 9. Paid Advertising

### 9.0 Weekly budget reality (₦50,000/week)

This marketing plan must be executable with a small, consistent spend.\n
**Budget constraint:** **₦50,000/week** (≈ ₦200,000/month).\n

**Rule:** at this budget, we do **1–2 channels at a time**, not all channels.\n
We optimize for **activation** (merchants connecting WhatsApp + Paystack + adding products) rather than raw traffic.

### 9.1 Channel Strategy

#### Recommended split (₦50,000/week)

| Channel | Weekly Budget | Goal | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Instagram/Facebook Ads | ₦35,000 | Signups + retargeting | Best for short demo videos + lookalikes |
| TikTok Ads | ₦15,000 | Awareness + cheap clicks | Use UGC-style demos + captions |

**Optional swap (if search intent is strong):** move ₦10,000/week from TikTok to **Google Search** for “WhatsApp selling tool Nigeria” keywords.

| Channel | Objective | Monthly Budget | Target CPA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram/Facebook Ads | Signups, awareness | ₦500K-1M | ₦5,000 |
| Google Search | High-intent capture | ₦300K-500K | ₦8,000 |
| TikTok Ads | Awareness, signups | ₦200K-400K | ₦4,000 |
| Twitter/X Ads | Awareness, engagement | ₦100K-200K | ₦6,000 |

> The table above reflects an “at scale” budget. For launch, use the weekly split table.

### 9.2 Ad Creative Strategy

**Winning Formats (Nigerian market):**
1. Screen recordings of AI agent conversations (highest CTR)
2. Merchant testimonial videos with real revenue numbers
3. Side-by-side comparison: "Without Vayva vs. With Vayva"
4. Pain-point-first: "Tired of losing customers because you can't respond fast enough?"
5. Social proof: "Join 500+ Nigerian merchants already selling with AI"

### 9.3 Retargeting Strategy
- Website visitors: Show demo video ad (7-day window)
- Started signup but did not complete: "Your AI agent is waiting" (14-day window)
- Free trial users nearing expiry: Conversion-focused ads with urgency
- Churned merchants: Win-back campaigns with new features

### 12.4 Weekly operating cadence (launch)

**Monday (setup + creative):**
- Build 2 ad variants (same offer, different hook)\n

- Reallocate spend to best CTR + best activation\n

**Friday (report):**
- Track: spend, clicks, signups, activated stores, CAC, activation rate\n
- Decide next week’s single experiment (one change only)
