## 1. Executive Summary

Vayva's go-to-market strategy targets Nigerian SMBs who sell primarily through WhatsApp and social media. Our approach is channel-native (we acquire merchants where they already sell), product-led (the AI agent demonstrates value within minutes), and community-driven (merchants become advocates). We aim to reach 1,000 active merchants by end of Year 1 and 10,000 by end of Year 2.

---

## 2. Market Analysis

### 2.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM)

Nigeria's e-commerce market exceeds **$12 billion** annually and is projected to reach $30 billion by 2030 (Statista, McKinsey Africa reports). The broader informal commerce economy -- which includes WhatsApp selling, Instagram commerce, and market trading -- is estimated at 3-5x the formal e-commerce figure.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Nigeria e-commerce market (2025) | $12B+ |
| Projected CAGR | 15-20% |
| Number of MSMEs in Nigeria | 41.5 million (SMEDAN) |
| MSMEs with online presence | ~5 million |
| WhatsApp business users (Nigeria) | 50M+ daily active |

### 2.2 Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

Our SAM consists of Nigerian SMBs that:
- Actively sell products or services online (via WhatsApp, Instagram, or websites)
- Generate ₦500,000+ in monthly revenue
- Have smartphone access and basic digital literacy
- Operate in our supported industry verticals

**Estimated SAM:** 2-3 million businesses representing ~$3 billion in annual transaction volume.

### 2.3 Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

Our realistic Year 1-3 target:
- **Year 1:** 1,000 merchants (₦300M ARR at blended ₦25K ARPU)
- **Year 2:** 10,000 merchants (₦4.2B ARR at blended ₦35K ARPU)
- **Year 3:** 50,000 merchants (₦24B ARR at blended ₦40K ARPU)

### 2.4 Market Dynamics

**Why now:**
1. **WhatsApp penetration is at critical mass.** Over 90% of Nigerian internet users use WhatsApp. It is the de facto commerce channel.
2. **AI costs have collapsed.** GPT-4o Mini at $0.375/M tokens and Llama 3.3 at $0.21/M tokens make AI-powered commerce economically viable at ₦25,000/month price points.
3. **Paystack and fintech infrastructure matured.** Seamless Naira payments are now table stakes.
4. **Post-COVID digital adoption.** Nigerian merchants adopted digital selling during COVID but lack sophisticated tools to scale it.
5. **Competitor gap.** No player offers AI-native, WhatsApp-first commerce for Nigerian SMBs.

### 4.2 WhatsApp-Native Acquisition

Since our product is WhatsApp-first, our acquisition should be WhatsApp-first:

1. **WhatsApp Business Directory Mining** -- Identify active sellers in target verticals
2. **Community Infiltration** -- Join WhatsApp groups where merchants discuss business (vendor groups, market associations, industry communities)
3. **Demo-First Outreach** -- Send a message showing what their AI agent could look like, personalized to their business
4. **WhatsApp Status Campaigns** -- Encourage early merchants to share Vayva on their WhatsApp Status

## 5. Pricing Strategy Rationale

### 5.1 Price Point Analysis

| Tier | Price | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| STARTER ₦25,000/mo | Entry point | Below the cost of hiring a part-time sales assistant (₦40,000-60,000/mo). Positions AI as a cheaper, better alternative to human hiring. |
| PRO ₦35,000/mo | Growth tier | ₦10,000 delta for unlimited orders + AI autopilot. Merchants at this level are doing enough volume that the ROI is 10-50x. |
| PRO PLUS ₦50,000/mo | Power tier | Industry dashboards + visual workflows. For merchants managing complex operations. Still under ₦100K/mo -- far below enterprise pricing. |

### 5.2 Pricing Psychology
- **Anchor against hiring:** "₦25,000/mo for an AI agent that works 24/7, vs. ₦60,000/mo for a human who works 8 hours"
- **Free trial as hook:** 1 month free on STARTER removes all risk
- **Monthly billing only:** Nigerian SMBs prefer monthly; annual plans create cash flow pressure
- **Naira-native:** No USD conversion confusion or FX risk

### 5.3 Revenue Beyond Subscriptions
- **3% withdrawal fee** -- Aligned with merchant success (we only earn when they earn)
- **AI credit top-ups** -- Merchants who see AI value naturally buy more credits
- **Future:** Premium add-ons (advanced analytics, multi-location, API access)

## 7. Launch Plan

### 7.1 Phase 1: Closed Beta (Months 1-2)
- **Target:** 50 hand-picked merchants across 5 verticals (fashion, food, beauty, electronics, events)
- **Geography:** Lagos only
- **Goal:** Product-market fit validation, NPS > 40, daily active usage > 70%
- **Acquisition:** Direct outreach, personal networks, WhatsApp communities
- **Success Metric:** 80% of beta merchants convert to paid after free trial

### 7.2 Phase 2: Lagos Launch (Months 3-6)
- **Target:** 200 merchants
- **Channels:** WhatsApp marketing, Instagram ads, referral program launch
- **Goal:** Prove unit economics (LTV:CAC > 3x), validate acquisition channels
- **Key Activities:**
  - Launch referral program
  - First merchant success stories published
  - Instagram and TikTok content engine established
  - Partnership with 2-3 market associations

### 7.3 Phase 3: Nigeria Expansion (Months 6-12)
- **Target:** 1,000 merchants
- **Geography:** Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Ibadan, Kano
- **Channels:** All channels at scale, partnership network active
- **Goal:** ₦25M+ MRR, proven scalability
- **Key Activities:**
  - Paid social campaigns at scale
  - Industry-specific landing pages and campaigns
  - Ambassador program launched
  - Press coverage in TechCabal, Techpoint, Disrupt Africa

### 7.4 Phase 4: Scale & Expand (Year 2)
- **Target:** 10,000 merchants
- **Geography:** Nigeria nationwide + Ghana pilot
- **Channels:** Performance marketing, partnerships, brand
- **Goal:** ₦350M+ MRR, market leadership position
- **Key Activities:**
  - Enterprise/agency partnerships
  - Francophone West Africa research
  - Series A fundraise
  - Team scaling (20-30 employees)

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp API changes/restrictions | High | Medium | Multi-channel readiness (Instagram DM, SMS). Direct BSP partnership. |
| Price sensitivity (₦25K too high) | High | Medium | Strong ROI messaging. Free trial. Consider ₦15K micro-tier. |
| Competitor launches AI features | Medium | High | Execution speed. AI depth vs. AI checkbox. First-mover in Nigeria. |
| Naira devaluation impacts costs | Medium | High | Hedge AI compute costs. Local infrastructure where possible. |
| Merchant digital literacy gap | Medium | Medium | Simplified onboarding. WhatsApp-based support. Video tutorials in Pidgin. |
| Payment infrastructure reliability | Medium | Low | Paystack uptime is 99.9%+. Backup processor relationship. |
