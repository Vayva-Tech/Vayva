## Overview

Vayva serves Nigerian merchants across 20+ industry verticals. These personas represent our primary user segments, derived from market research and early adopter interviews. Each persona informs product decisions, feature prioritization, and marketing messaging.

## Persona 1: Amaka -- Fashion Retailer

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Name | Amaka Okafor |
| Age | 28 |
| Location | Lagos (Yaba) |
| Business | "Amaka's Closet" -- women's fashion and accessories |
| Business Size | Solo operator, 1 assistant |
| Monthly Revenue | NGN 800,000 -- NGN 1,500,000 |
| Tech Comfort | Moderate -- uses Instagram, WhatsApp, and Canva daily |
| Current Selling Channels | Instagram DMs, WhatsApp broadcasts, occasional pop-up markets |

### Goals

- Stop losing sales because she cannot reply to DMs fast enough
- Have a proper online store with product photos, sizes, and prices
- Automate order taking so she can focus on sourcing and styling
- Track which products sell best and which to restock
- Accept payments without chasing customers for bank transfers

### Pain Points

- Spends 4-6 hours daily replying to "How much?" messages on Instagram and WhatsApp
- Loses track of orders managed through screenshots and notebook entries
- Customers abandon orders because she takes too long to respond
- Cannot sell while sleeping -- loses overnight and weekend inquiries
- Payment collection is manual: bank transfers, then she must verify each one
- No inventory visibility -- oversells items that are already sold out

### How Vayva Solves This

| Pain Point | Vayva Solution |
|-----------|----------------|
| Manual message replies | AI Sales Agent handles WhatsApp inquiries 24/7, answers "How much?", shows product photos, and takes orders |
| Order tracking chaos | Centralized order management dashboard with status tracking |
| Lost overnight sales | AI agent works around the clock, capturing sales while Amaka sleeps |
| Manual payments | Paystack integration: customers pay directly, funds settle automatically |
| No inventory tracking | Real-time inventory management with low-stock alerts |
| No sales insights | Analytics dashboard showing top products, revenue trends, and customer behavior |

### Recommended Tier

**STARTER** (NGN 25,000/month) -- Amaka is a solo operator. She needs the AI agent, storefront, and order management. As she grows, she can upgrade to PRO for multi-staff and autopilot.

- Phone lines are constantly busy during lunch and dinner rush, losing orders

### How Vayva Solves This

| Pain Point | Vayva Solution |
|-----------|----------------|
| Busy phone lines | AI agent takes unlimited simultaneous orders via WhatsApp |
| Order errors | Customers select from the actual menu with prices; AI confirms before submission |
| No sales data | Restaurant-specific analytics: popular dishes, peak hours, average order value |
| Delivery chaos | Order queue with delivery status tracking and estimated times |
| Spam broadcasts | Campaign system with opt-in messaging and scheduled promotions |

### Recommended Tier

**PRO** (NGN 35,000/month) -- Emeka needs multi-staff access (3 seats), higher order volume (10,000/month), and industry-specific restaurant dashboards. AI Autopilot can handle menu optimization suggestions.

## Persona Usage Guidelines

### For Product Decisions

When evaluating a new feature, ask: "Which personas benefit from this? How does it rank in their goals and pain points?" Features that serve multiple personas should be prioritized.

### For Engineering

When building a feature, reference the relevant persona to understand the real-world use case. This helps make better UX decisions at the implementation level.

### For Marketing

Each persona maps to specific messaging angles, ad targeting criteria, and content themes. Use the personas' language and pain points in copy.

### For Sales

During demos, identify which persona the prospect most closely matches. Tailor the demo flow to highlight the features most relevant to their profile.
